Ganga River: The Lifeline of India

The Ganga River is one of the most important rivers of India in terms of its natural, cultural, economic, and environmental aspects. It is a lifeline of India as it feeds more than 600 million populations of its upstream and downstream basins. It has significant cultural importance for the Hindus. The river is home to approximately 140 species of fish, 90 species of amphibians, and also reptiles and mammals, including critically endangered species such as the gharial and South Asian river dolphin.
